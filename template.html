MLA Style Guide (9th Ed.)

Welcome to the Modern Language Association (MLA) quick guide. This page covers the basics of formatting your papers and citing your sources. Remember: the goal of citation is to help your reader find the material you used.

1. Basic Paper Formatting

Before you even type a word, ensure your document is set up correctly. This prevents last-minute panic when trying to adjust margins.

  • 1-inch margins on all sides.
  • Double-spaced text throughout.
  • Times New Roman, 12pt font.

2. In-Text Citations

MLA uses a parenthetical author-page method. This means the author's last name and the page number(s) from which the quotation or paraphrase is taken must appear in the text.

Standard Format:
Romantic poetry often explores the "spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings" (Wordsworth 263).

3. Works Cited Page

Your list of works cited should appear at the end of your paper. It provides the information necessary for a reader to locate and retrieve any source you cite in the body of the paper.

Book Format:
Last Name, First Name. Title of Book. City of Publication, Publisher, Publication Date.
Journal Article:
Author(s). "Title of Article." Title of Journal, Volume, Issue, Year, pages.
