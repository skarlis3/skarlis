Example Essay Organization

This is a flexible framework — all of these sections are required, but the order and length can be adapted to fit your genre. Some sections may be longer than shown here.

A Note on Organization

This paper works best with a broad to narrow approach. Think of it as an hourglass:

  • Broad at the start: overview of the professional field and the genre you plan to analyze
  • Narrow in the middle: detailed analysis of the genre — audience, purpose, and at least three conventions
  • Broad at the end: how the genre functions in professional practice, then wrap up in a conclusion

Example Outline

Introduction

Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)

Option A — Two-paragraph intro:

  1. Paragraph 1: Introduce the professional field (paralegal/legal work) and the types of writing that happen in it.
  2. Paragraph 2: Narrow to the specific genre you're analyzing (IRAC briefs). State that this is the focus of your essay (this is your thesis).

Option B — One-paragraph intro:

Introduce the field and the genre in one paragraph. Best if the professional context doesn't need a lot of setup.

Body Paragraphs

Genre Description, Purpose, & Audience (1–3 paragraphs)

  • Provide an overview and description of the genre.
  • Discuss the purpose — why this genre exists in legal practice.
  • Analyze the intended audience and how they shape the genre.
These can be covered in one paragraph or spread across 2–3 paragraphs, depending on how much you have to say. The description should come early in the paper, but purpose and audience could also work later.

Convention #1 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick one convention (common feature) of the genre to analyze.
  • Describe the convention and use evidence from your samples to support your point.
  • Analyze how this convention makes the genre effective for its purpose.

Convention #2 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick another convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

Convention #3 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick a third convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

How the Genre Functions in Professional Practice (1–2 paragraphs)

Pick one or two of the following approaches (or come up with your own):

  • Professional Values: What does this genre reveal about what the legal field values? How does its structure reflect those values?
  • Professional Communication: How does this genre connect to other forms of communication in legal practice? What role does it play in a larger workflow?
  • What Happens When Conventions Aren't Followed: What would happen if someone didn't follow these conventions? What does that tell you about why they matter?
  • Something else: If you have another angle on how the genre functions in the field, go for it.
Conclusion

Conclusion (1 paragraph)

  • Summarize your key findings from the analysis.
  • Restate how the genre contributes to the goals and functioning of professional legal practice.
