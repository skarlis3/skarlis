ENGL 1181 — Independent Study

Getting Started — Start here for important info about how the class works and what to do in the first few weeks.

I split the first week's work into Weeks 1 and 2 since it's heavier on readings and videos. Week 2 falls over spring break, so all of this just needs to be done before we meet on Tuesday, March 17 — work on it next week or over break, whatever fits your schedule best.

Week 1: Genre Introduction to genre — what it means, how it works, and why it matters for writing. Week 2: Rhetoric & Field Exploration How purpose, audience, and context shape writing. Start exploring your field's genres. Week 3: Genre Selection Narrow down to one genre, find a sample, and start analyzing its rhetorical situation. Week 4: Sample Collection & Conventions Collect multiple samples of your genre and start identifying its conventions. Week 5: Prewriting & First Drafts Read about shitty first drafts, get started on your genre analysis essay, and submit your partial draft. Week 6: Full Rough Draft Complete your genre analysis essay using conference feedback. Readings on TED paragraphs and MLA basics. Week 7: Visual Rhetoric & Final Portfolio Learn about visual rhetoric and multimodal texts. Introduction to the final portfolio. Week 8: Final Draft Submit your final revision of the genre analysis essay. Week 9: Final Reflection Build and submit your multimodal final reflection.
