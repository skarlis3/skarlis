Reflection 3: Genre Selection & Initial Observations

Submit on Canvas · Graded: ✓ system

This reflection has two parts. You can submit both parts on the same page. You will also need to upload a screenshot or PDF of your genre sample — see How to Save a Sample as a PDF below.

Part 1: Choosing Your Genre

Look back at the genres you found during your Week 2 field exploration. Choose one genre that you'll focus on for the rest of the semester — this is the genre you'll analyze in your genre analysis essay.

Which genre did you choose? Describe what this type of document is — what does it do, and what role does it play in the field? Why did you choose this genre over the others you found?

Part 2: Looking at a Sample

Find one real sample of the genre you chose. This could be a template, an example from a professional organization, a sample from a training resource — anything that shows you what this genre actually looks like in practice.

Once you have your sample, look at it and respond to the following:

  • Purpose: What is this document designed to do? What is it trying to accomplish?
  • Audience: Who is the primary reader of this document? Are there secondary audiences — other people who might read it even though it isn't written directly for them?
  • Context: Where and when does this document show up? What situation leads to it being created or used?

Your answers for Part 2 can be in list form — you don't need to write full paragraphs.

Submit as a Word document or text entry on Canvas. Upload your genre sample as a separate file — either a screenshot (image) or a PDF. If your sample is a webpage, see How to Save a Sample as a PDF below for instructions.

How to Save a Sample as a PDF

If your genre sample is a webpage or online document, you can save it as a PDF using your browser's print function:

  1. Open the page with your genre sample in your browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, etc.).
  2. Open Print: Press Ctrl + P (Windows) or Cmd + P (Mac). You can also go to the menu and select Print.
  3. Change the destination: Where it says "Printer" or "Destination," click the dropdown and select "Save as PDF" (or "Microsoft Print to PDF").
  4. Click Save and choose where to save the file on your computer.
  5. Upload the PDF to Canvas along with your reflection.

If you're on a phone or tablet and can't print to PDF, a screenshot works too — just make sure the full document is visible and readable.
