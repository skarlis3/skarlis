Final Reflection

ENGL 1181 — Due: Monday, May 5

The Final Reflection is a reflection on your growth, strengths, and learning this semester. You'll curate portfolio artifacts from your work — excerpts, screenshots, before/after comparisons — and reflect on what they show about you as a writer and student. This is separate from your final genre analysis essay, which is submitted on its own.

This is a multimodal project. Your reflection should demonstrate an understanding of visual rhetoric — the design choices you make (layout, color, spacing, images, how you present artifacts) should be intentional and purposeful, not just default slides with text. Complete the readings and videos before you start building.

Format Options

Choose one:

  1. PowerPoint or Google Slides — Build your reflection as a slide deck.
  2. Google Site (or another website) — Build your reflection as a webpage. If you use something other than Google Sites, just make sure I can access it via a link and that you can embed documents or screenshots.

Want to use a different format? Check with me first.

What Are Portfolio Artifacts?

Portfolio artifacts are pieces of evidence from your own work in this class — anything that demonstrates something about your writing, learning, or growth. This could be a before/after snippet from your genre analysis essay, notes from our meetings, a screenshot of your planning or revision process, your sample collection observations, or anything else from your work this semester.

What to Include

Your Final Reflection should cover all six of the following. See the Reflection Prompts page for full descriptions of each one.

What You Made

Sections 1–4 each require portfolio artifact(s) and a reflection.

  1. Growth as a Writer (Before & After) — Artifact required
  2. A Strength (Artifact + Reflection) — Artifact required
  3. Revision (Before & After) — Artifact required
  4. Growth as a Student (Flexible) — Artifact required

What You Think

Sections 5–6 are written responses — no artifacts needed.

  1. Advice for Future Students — Written response only
  2. Final Thoughts — Written response only

Grading

The Final Reflection is worth 15% of your course grade.

Grade: A (95%)

The Final Reflection is thoughtful and well developed, with well-chosen artifacts that clearly demonstrate the points being made. All six sections are present and substantive. The reflection demonstrates an understanding of visual rhetoric through intentional, purposeful design choices.

Grade: B (85%)

The reflection meets assignment requirements, but one area isn't quite working — for example, artifacts that don't clearly connect to the reflections, sections that stay surface-level, or design choices that feel default rather than intentional.

Grade: C (75%)

The reflection meets the basic assignment requirements but has multiple areas that aren't fully working. The work is present but doesn't fully deliver on the criteria described above.

Grade: D (65%)

An attempt at the reflection is clear, but there are major issues. Sections may be missing or underdeveloped, artifacts may not connect to the reflections, or the visual presentation shows little intentional design.

Grade: E

The reflection does not meet the assignment requirements or has critical gaps.

An E grade will fall between 0% and 55% depending on the extent of the missing or unmet requirements.

No Credit (0%)

The final reflection was not submitted.

Note: An A receives a 95%. Scores above 95% are reserved for work that demonstrates exceptional effort above and beyond the criteria described above.
