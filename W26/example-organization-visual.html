Example Essay Organization

This is a flexible framework — all of these sections are required, but the order and length can be adapted to fit your genre. Some sections may be longer than shown here.

The Hourglass Shape

Your essay works best with a broad → narrow → broad structure:

Broad: Professional field & genre intro
▼
Narrower: Purpose & audience
▼
Narrowest: Convention analysis
▼
Broader: Genre function in practice
▼
Broad: Conclusion

Example Outline

1 Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)

Introduce the professional field and the genre you will analyze.

Option A: Two paragraphs

Paragraph 1: Introduce the paralegal/legal field and the types of writing that happen in it.

Paragraph 2: Narrow to IRAC briefs specifically. State that this is the focus of your essay (this is your thesis).

Option B: One paragraph

Combine the field overview and genre intro into one paragraph. Best if the professional context doesn't need a lot of setup.

2 Genre Description, Purpose, & Audience (1–3 paragraphs)

  • Provide an overview and description of the genre.
  • Discuss the purpose — why this genre exists in legal practice.
  • Analyze the audience and how they shape the genre.
These can be one paragraph or 2–3, depending on how much you have to say. The description should come early, but purpose and audience could also work later in the paper.

3 Convention #1 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick one convention (common feature) to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples to support your point.
  • Analyze how it makes the genre effective for its purpose.

4 Convention #2 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick another convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.

5 Convention #3 (1 paragraph)

  • Pick a third convention to analyze.
  • Describe it and use evidence from your samples.
  • Analyze its effectiveness.
Three conventions is the minimum. If you notice a fourth convention worth analyzing, add another paragraph — you are not limited to three.

6 How the Genre Functions in Professional Practice (1–2 paragraphs)

Pick one or two of the following approaches, or come up with your own:

Professional Values

What does this genre reveal about what the legal field values? How does its structure reflect those values?

Professional Communication

How does this genre connect to other writing in legal practice? What role does it play in a larger workflow?

Breaking Conventions

What would happen if someone didn't follow these conventions? What does that tell you?

Something Else

If you have another angle on how the genre functions in the field, go for it.

You don't need to cover all of these. Pick what makes sense for your genre.

7 Conclusion (1 paragraph)

  • Summarize your key findings from the analysis.
  • Restate how the genre contributes to the goals and functioning of professional legal practice.
