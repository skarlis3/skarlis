Getting Started

ENGL 1181 — Independent Study

Welcome to ENGL 1181. This independent study is set up a little differently than a typical class — we'll meet once a week, and you'll work through readings, videos, and writing assignments on your own between meetings. This guide covers how the class works and what to do first.

How This Class Works

The class officially starts the week of March 3. Each week, you'll have online work to complete (videos, readings, and a writing assignment) plus a weekly in-person meeting with me. The online work is due Sundays at 11:59pm, and we'll go over it together at our Tuesday meeting, where I'll give you feedback and we'll talk through the next steps.

The meetings are the core of this class — they're where you get feedback, ask questions, and work through ideas. If something comes up and you can't make a meeting, email me so we can set up a Zoom or alternate time. With only one hour a week, missing a session means losing a full week.

A Note About Grading

Most of what you write in this class — especially early on — is graded on a check system. I'm looking at whether you engaged with the prompt and put in genuine thought, not whether your grammar is perfect. The reflections and shorter assignments are a space for you to think on paper. Write in your own voice, be honest about what you're noticing, and don't worry about sounding polished.

Before Our First Meeting

Our first meeting is Tuesday, March 17. Before that meeting, you'll need to complete the work in Week 1 and Week 2 (see the Weekly Assignments page for all handouts). I split the first week's work into two weeks since it's heavier on readings and videos — Week 2 falls over spring break, so you can work on it next week or over break, whatever fits your schedule.
