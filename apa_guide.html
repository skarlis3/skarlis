APA Style Guide (7th Ed.)

Welcome to the APA quick guide. While MLA is common in the humanities, APA (American Psychological Association) is the standard for social sciences. It emphasizes the date of publication because currency is crucial in these fields.

1. Basic Paper Formatting

Unlike MLA, APA requires a separate Title Page. Do not put the title on the first page of your essay text; put it on its own page at the start.

  • Title Page: Includes Paper Title, Author Name, Affiliation (Department/College), Course Number/Name, Instructor Name, and Due Date centered in the upper half of the page.
  • Page Numbers: Top right corner (starting on the title page).
  • Line Spacing: Double-spaced throughout.

2. In-Text Citations

APA uses the Author-Date method. If you quote directly, you must also add the page number.

Paraphrase:
Research suggests that sleep deprivation negatively affects cognitive performance (Smith, 2020).
Direct Quote:
Smith (2020) notes that "the lack of REM sleep causes immediate deficits" (p. 45).

3. References: Books & Reports

Note: In APA, only the first word of the title, the first word of a subtitle, and proper nouns are capitalized (Sentence Case).

Print Book:
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle. Publisher.

Example:
Gladwell, M. (2008). Outliers: The story of success. Little, Brown and Company.
Online Book / E-Book:
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work. Publisher. URL or DOI.

Example:
Damasio, A. (2010). Self comes to mind: Constructing the conscious brain. Pantheon. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2010.11.001
Report or White Paper:
Organization Name. (Year). Title of report. URL

Example:
National Institute of Mental Health. (2021). Digital technology and mental health. https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/digital-tech

4. References: Articles (Journals & News)

Journal Article (Online):
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Periodical, Volume(Issue), pages. URL or DOI.

Example:
McCauley, S. M., & Christiansen, M. H. (2019). Language learning as language use. Psychological Review, 126(1), 1â€“51. https://doi.org/10.1037/rev0000126
News Article (Online):
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Title of Publication. URL

Example:
Weir, K. (2017, January 5). Kristin Stump makes the case for forensic science. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/...

5. Social Media & Online Communities

For social media, if the author's real name is known, use it followed by the username in brackets. If only the username is known, use that without brackets.

Social Media Post (TikTok, X/Twitter, Instagram):
Author, A. A. [@username]. (Year, Month Day). Content of the post up to the first 20 words [Description of audiovisuals]. Site Name. URL

Example:
Washington Post [@washingtonpost]. (2022, May 3). Newsrooms are changing the way they cover climate change [Video]. TikTok. https://www.tiktok.com/@washingtonpost/video/7093
Blog Post (Substack, Medium, Personal Blog):
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of post. Blog Name. URL

Example:
Newport, C. (2023, February 15). On the value of deep work. Study Hacks Blog. https://calnewport.com/blog/
Online Forum (Reddit, Hacker News):
Username. (Year, Month Day). Title of post [Online forum post]. Site Name. URL

Example:
DeepSkyDiver. (2023, March 12). Is there a consensus on the best telescope for beginners? [Online forum post]. Reddit. https://www.reddit.com/r/astronomy/comments/12345/
