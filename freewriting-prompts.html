Freewriting Prompts

A collection of prompts for our daily 10-minute warm-ups. These are designed to get the gears turning, clear the static, and bridge the gap between thinking and writing.

1. The Base Prompt (Open-Ended)

Use this when we need to just clear our heads.

The Brain Dump Write about whatever is in your head right nowâ€”stress, lunch plans, traffic, or your to-do list. If you get stuck, write "I don't know what to write" until a new thought comes. The only rule is: don't stop moving your hand/fingers.

2. Writer Identity

Prompts to explore how we see ourselves as students and writers.

The "Good Writer" Myth Describe your definition of a "good writer." Do they use big words? Do they write fast? Do you fit that description? Why or why not?
The Memory Describe one specific memory you have about writing in school (positive or negative). How does that memory affect how you feel when you sit down to write today?
Honest Assessment Finish this sentence and keep writing: "If I were being honest about how I feel about writing/this class, I would say..."

3. GenAI & Technology

Critical thinking about the intersection of humanity and automation.

The AI Apology If a friend sent you a heartfelt apology letter but you found out they used AI to write it, would it still count? Why or why not? Does the origin matter if the words are perfect?
The Human Role If AI eventually does 90% of the writing in the world, what is left for humans to do? Is there any value in a messy, human first draft?
Analog vs. Digital Compare how your brain works when typing on a phone versus writing by hand on paper. Do you think differently depending on the tool? Which makes it easier to be honest?

4. Communication

Thinking about audience, tone, and clarity.

Code Switching Write a few sentences describing your weekend to your best friend. Now, describe the same weekend to a strict boss. What specifically changed in your vocabulary or tone?
The Misunderstanding Write about a time a text or email you sent was misunderstood. What specifically went wrong (tone, word choice, lack of context)?
