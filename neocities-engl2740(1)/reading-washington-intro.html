This collection includes key excerpts from Booker T. Washington's autobiography Up From Slavery (1901), which chronicles his journey from enslavement to becoming one of the most influential African American leaders of the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Washington founded the Tuskegee Institute and advocated for industrial education and economic self-reliance as a path to racial progress. His famous Atlanta Exposition Address (1895) outlined his philosophy of accommodation and gradualism, which would later draw criticism from W.E.B. Du Bois and others.
